<section name="46fc" class="section section--body section--first"><div class="section-divider"><hr class="section-divider"></div><div class="section-content"><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn"><h3 name="9ae3" id="9ae3" class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title">5 Years of Mozilla Community Space Taipei - A Brief Status</h3><h4 name="34e2" id="34e2" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--h3 graf--subtitle">May 2014 - 2019</h4><blockquote name="eaa3" id="eaa3" class="graf graf--blockquote graf-after--h4">(This is the updated version to <a href="/2017/03/mozilla-community-space-taipei-status.html" data-href="/2017/03/mozilla-community-space-taipei-status.html" class="markup--anchor markup--blockquote-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">this report</a> from 2017)<br>Download the full slide <a href="https://www.icloud.com/keynote/0ZYneqMJqaGOG6f-2lXPnClnw#Community_Space_Taipei_-_status_report_-_201905" data-href="https://www.icloud.com/keynote/0ZYneqMJqaGOG6f-2lXPnClnw#Community_Space_Taipei_-_status_report_-_201905" class="markup--anchor markup--blockquote-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">here</a>.</blockquote><p name="9cc1" id="9cc1" class="graf graf--p graf-after--blockquote">Mozilla Community Space Taipei ”摩茲工寮” is an event / meeting / co-working space and shelter to the local Mozillians, Mozilla volunteers, and OSS communities.</p><p name="0a9b" id="0a9b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">It is the first of this kinds of Mozilla space run by community (and another one is <a href="http://www.mozilla.or.id/space.html" data-href="http://www.mozilla.or.id/space.html" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Jakarta Space</a>). These spaces are leading by Mozilla Open Innovation Team, managing by local MozTW Mozillian volunteers.</p><p name="51ec" id="51ec" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Here I will share some brief status of the Taipei Space.</p><p name="b437" id="b437" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">(I’ll use “MozTW Space” or “Space” as the abbreviation to “Mozilla Community Space Taipei” below.)</p><figure name="24ce" id="24ce" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*wALKgzfKv_SADuy70dYVUw.png" data-width="1024" data-height="768" data-is-featured="true" src="/assets/images/miro.medium.com/26d0ca5f824c24b3.png"></figure><h3 name="8fea" id="8fea" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--figure">MozTW Community</h3><p name="5542" id="5542" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Mozilla Taiwan Community (MozTW) is a local Mozilla volunteer community. We are contributing localizing, promoting Firefox, Mozilla, and open cultures to Mozilla and open source projects since 2004.</p><figure name="e5be" id="e5be" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="0*BEn-wS3bhSjjzlbX.png" data-width="1024" data-height="767" src="/assets/images/cdn-images-1.medium.com/4187a49459080260.png"></figure><p name="37f5" id="37f5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">MozTW maintained an online <a href="https://moztw.org/" data-href="https://moztw.org/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">product portal and forum</a>, hosting <a href="https://moztw.org/events/" data-href="https://moztw.org/events/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">many offline events</a> and engaging with other Asia Mozillians and open source hackers.</p><figure name="29c5" id="29c5" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="0*uZxoo5Gk0ih51KJV.png" data-width="1024" data-height="767" src="/assets/images/cdn-images-1.medium.com/4ffcd0deebfe17b2.png"></figure><h3 name="f6db" id="f6db" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--figure">History of MozTW Community Space</h3><p name="cb23" id="cb23" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">The Community Space was an idea initially raised by William Quiviger in 2013, and sponsored by WPR team. We started the first experimental space in Taipei spring 2014.</p><p name="55b8" id="55b8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">After April 2016, the space project had transitioned to Participation Team, supported by Brian King. In February 2017, it was continuous with Open Innovation Group with the help of George Roter and Konstantina until now.</p><figure name="455d" id="455d" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*87yN-jqbkBDcRprLUk6CNg.png" data-width="1024" data-height="768" src="/assets/images/miro.medium.com/41dfb380a2004057.png"></figure><figure name="cba0" id="cba0" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--figure"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*0o-EYktMcwXq6PiyT11Rmg.png" data-width="1024" data-height="768" src="/assets/images/miro.medium.com/83cce0dac6eda771.png"></figure><h3 name="b7b8" id="b7b8" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--figure">Keyholders</h3><p name="9815" id="9815" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">MozTW Space is co-managing by “Keyholders,” 18 local Mozilla volunteers.</p><p name="d3a4" id="d3a4" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Keyholders are all vouched Mozillians and have the same privilege. People need to be countersigned by more than 50% of Mozillian visitors (in the last three months) when they applied for the position.</p><p name="a783" id="a783" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The threshold represents the community’s support to the applicant.</p><figure name="fd9f" id="fd9f" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*KSN7A86Uhl0O8K0NM9O72Q.png" data-width="1024" data-height="768" src="/assets/images/miro.medium.com/9b77945d9859a7dc.png"></figure><h3 name="cf29" id="cf29" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--figure">Visitors &amp; Events</h3><p name="ea75" id="ea75" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Everyone is welcome to visiting MozTW space when there is any Keyholder inside. However, it’s not opening 24 hours / 7 days a week due to the volunteers’ ability. In the last five years, we managed to open the space 22 days per month on average. (Part of the weeknight and most day time of weekend)</p><p name="55aa" id="55aa" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Besides Mozilla events, we also welcome and support all open culture communities to hosting their activities in the MozTW Space. Totally there are more than <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">16000 visitors</strong> and <strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">1256 events/meetups</strong> since soft-opening of May 2014.</p><figure name="d856" id="d856" class="graf graf--figure graf-after--p"><img class="graf-image" data-image-id="1*4v_DLXDU7itsozd2yQ360w.png" data-width="1024" data-height="768" src="/assets/images/miro.medium.com/5e62d94176c04217.png"></figure><p name="b935" id="b935" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">The communities that hosted the events at space came from a very diverse domain, includes open source projects, programming, AI, blockchain, policy advocacy, civil society hackers, CS education, open culture, open data, D&amp;I, infused, makers &amp; designers.</p><p name="1dc6" id="1dc6" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Some of the closest cooperate communities including Rust Taiwan, <a href="http://g0v.tw/" data-href="http://g0v.tw/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">G0V</a>, <a href="http://openstreetmap.tw/" data-href="http://openstreetmap.tw/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">OSM</a>, <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Wikimedia_Taiwan" data-href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Wikimedia_Taiwan" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Wikimedia Taiwan</a>, <a href="http://creativecommons.tw/%3Cbr%3E" data-href="http://creativecommons.tw/%3Cbr%3E" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Creative Commons Taiwan</a>, <a href="https://www.ubuntu-tw.org/" data-href="https://www.ubuntu-tw.org/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Ubuntu TW</a>, <a href="http://ocf.tw/" data-href="http://ocf.tw/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Open Culture Foundation</a>, <a href="http://sitcon.org/" data-href="http://sitcon.org/" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">SITCON</a> (Students Information Technology Conference) and <a href="http://coscup.org" data-href="http://coscup.org" class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">COSCUP</a> (Conference for Open Source Coders, Users &amp;
